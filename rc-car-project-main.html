A fully custom-designed and 3D-printed remote-controlled car built around the Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32-C3. The chassis was modelled from scratch in Fusion 360 and printed on a Creality Ender-3 S1 Pro. Drive is handled by a small 3 V hobby DC motor controlled via a TB6612FNG dual H-bridge, while steering uses an SG90-class servo for Ackermann-style front-wheel control.

Wireless control is achieved via ESP-NOW between two ESP32-C3 boards — one on the car (receiver) and one on the handheld controller (transmitter). PWM motor control uses the LEDC API with joystick dead zones for precise low-speed handling.

A full custom PCB was designed in EasyEDA consolidating the XIAO ESP32-C3, TB6612FNG motor driver, servo connector, power switch, decoupling capacitors, and an LED power indicator onto a single board sized to fit the chassis. PCB is designed and ready for JLCPCB manufacture.

CAD Design & 3D Printing

The full chassis was designed parametrically in Fusion 360 as a multi-body assembly with separate components for the main body, front steering knuckles, wheel mounts, motor bracket, and servo bracket. All geometry was designed to integrate directly with the SG90 servo geometry and standard RC hobby wheels.

Phase 1 — Fusion 360 Design
Full chassis assembly with 4 wheels, motor mount, servo bracket, and electronics bay
The chassis was designed as a complete parametric assembly in Fusion 360. Components include: main chassis plate, front axle steering knuckles for Ackermann geometry, rear motor bracket sized to the 3 V hobby DC motor, SG90 servo bracket with horn clearance, four wheel mounts, and a top electronics bay for the PCB and LiPo cell. All tolerances were set to account for FDM dimensional variation on the Ender-3 S1 Pro.
Phase 2 — 3D Printing on Ender-3 S1 Pro
PLA print with focus on front steering section and wheel cutout geometry
All chassis parts printed in PLA on the Creality Ender-3 S1 Pro. The front steering section with wheel cutouts required careful orientation to print without supports while maintaining dimensional accuracy for the servo horn interface. Layer height set to 0.2 mm for structural parts, 0.15 mm for steering knuckles requiring tighter tolerances. Parts were test-fitted before electronics installation.

Build & Assembly

Phase 1 — Bench Testing
Motor, servo, and driver validated independently before chassis mounting
Before final assembly, each subsystem was bench-tested independently. The TB6612FNG motor driver was tested for bidirectional PWM control with the XIAO ESP32-C3 using both LEDC channels. The SG90 servo was validated for full range of motion on GPIO D1. Drive motor direction and speed control were confirmed functional at varying PWM duty cycles. All bench tests used LiPo power through a bench PSU current limiter for safety.
Phase 2 — Chassis Assembly
3D-printed chassis assembled with motor, servo, and wheel mounts
All 3D-printed chassis parts assembled in sequence: rear motor bracket press-fitted and secured, front steering knuckles inserted with press-fit axle pins, SG90 servo mounted in its bracket with horn connected to the steering linkage, and all four wheels attached. Mechanical fit and steering range of motion verified before electronics installation.
Phase 3 — Electronics Mounting
XIAO ESP32-C3, TB6612FNG breakout, and LiPo mounted to chassis top plate
Electronics mounted directly to the chassis top plate using double-sided tape and standoffs. XIAO ESP32-C3 positioned for USB-C access. TB6612FNG breakout wired to all motor and GPIO pins per pin mapping table. LiPo cell positioned for centre-of-gravity balance. Full wireless control test confirmed with ESP-NOW transmitter board — car drives and steers correctly in both directions.

Next Steps

PCB Manufacture & Testing — JLCPCB
Submit the completed EasyEDA PCB design to JLCPCB for 2-layer fabrication. Assemble the custom PCB and validate all subsystems: motor driver PWM, servo control, power supply, and ESP-NOW wireless link. Replace the current breakout-board wiring with the clean PCB-mounted solution.
Handheld Transmitter Controller Build
Build a handheld joystick transmitter using a second XIAO ESP32-C3 with the ESP-NOW TX firmware. The controller will feature an analog joystick for throttle/steering and a 3D-printed ergonomic enclosure designed in Fusion 360 to fit the hand comfortably.
Body Shell — Fusion 360 & 3D Print
Design a full body shell enclosure in Fusion 360 that mounts over the electronics chassis — styled to look like a scaled car body. The shell clips onto the chassis top plate and includes ventilation slots for the motor and power electronics. Printed in PLA with optional colour accent pieces.
PWM & Dead Zone Tuning
Profile optimal PWM frequency and dead zone parameters for low-speed control precision and reliable direction switching. Current firmware uses hardcoded dead zones — a runtime tuning mode will allow configuring these via the transmitter joystick with values stored in EEPROM.
